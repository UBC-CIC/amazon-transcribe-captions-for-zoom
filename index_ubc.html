Closed Captioning for Zoom

This page enables closed captioning for live Zoom sessions. For help with using Zoom generally, check out UBC’s Zoom instructor guide.

How do I add closed captioning to Zoom?

Once you start a Zoom session, you will copy an identifier for the session (called a token) and paste it in the form below. This token tells the tool which Zoom session it should send the captions to. Then you can start the captioning, and students will be able to read whatever your microphone hears in real time.

> Use real-time closed captioning with Zoom

Use real-time closed captioning with Zoom

  1. This page runs the tool that captions your audio, so please make sure this page is open in a browser window you won't accidentally close during lecture.
  2. In your active Zoom session, click the Closed Captioning icon at the bottom of your screen, and select Copy the API token.
  3. Paste the token in the form below and set the language you'll be speaking in.
  4. Click Start below, when you are ready. Give your browser permission to access your microphone, and the captioning will begin.
    • Tell students to turn captioning on by clicking the arrow next to the Closed Captioning icon, and selecting Show Subtitle. Note there may be a slight delay between you starting the captioning and this arrow appearing.
  5. Click Stop to pause or end captioning. When you end the Zoom session, the transcript will download automatically to your computer as a text file, in a folder created for the session.

Tips

  • The captioning tool will only caption audio heard by your microphone. It will not caption other participants, unless you use speakers so your microphone can hear them. (However, using speakers is not recommended, since it is likely to create audio problems.)
  • Muting your microphone in Zoom will not mute your microphone for the captioning tool. To "mute" captioning, you will need to click stop below.
